IT Works

I had the opportunity to interview a friend who is currently working in the information technology industry and is working in New Zealand. I called to interview her and below is my conversation about the interview:

Me: Tell us about your IT job please. What are you doing exactly?

Thao: I am working as a junior developer in a team. Normally, a team leader assigns me to develop/create a feature and I code for that.

Me: Please let us know about the company you are working for.

Thao: My company is about providing Computer Science people a place for upskilling. We are creating an online platform as a community for developers to learn and share knowledge.

Me: What other types of work are you supposed to do?

Thao: I have responsibility for developing a product’s features, meanwhile having meetings with my teammates, and writing reports.

Me: Who are all the various people in your workplace with whom you interact? Tell us about them please.

Thao: Most of the time, I interact with my beloved laptop and other developers in my team. There is a product owner who allocate tasks to us, a scrum master who has high skill, and other developers who work together to create a project.

Me: Please tell us about your interactions with other IT professionals.

Thao: Working as a developer, I usually contact with other teammates through comments from our source code, meetings, and text if it is necessary. As we work during the pandemic, Zoom is our online tool for team meeting.

Me: What about your interactions with clients or investors?

Thao: Since we are programmers, we rarely interact with our clients or stakeholders. An analyst or the product owner in the team will help us doing that.

Me: What elements of your career are you spending the most time on? Tell us about these, please.

Thao: Most of the time, I code for a feature, review code from other developers, and meeting.

Me: What aspects of your job are most difficult for you?

Thao: In fact, I sit all day in a chair and type whatever I think it works. Also, I need to keep learning new technologies and think how to improve the product. When you love something, it is none of challenging.

Me: Finally, can you share an instance of the work you do that best captures the IT industry's essence?

Thao: The most challenging thing for me is not how hard the work is, but how difficult to work with people. I used to have a teammate who crashed our work and a developer who was a little bit egotistical.
