Area 1: Cloud computing

Cloud computing has grown as a globally known model for service-oriented computing, where cloud computing and solutions are provided as a service. [1]. Cloud computing is the most advanced security with the aim that users can use this technology with peace of mind. One of the biggest challenges to its widespread adoption was the security and privacy issue, taking into account the significant business and technological benefits of the cloud. The owners ultimately relinquish exclusive control over their data, particularly for outsourced data services, to the CSPs [2]. Google's new privacy policy, for example suggests that they practically can treat uploaded user data arbitrarily [3]. Consequently, if their outsourced data includes confidential personal data such as bank and clinical information and social media platform accounts, from the point of view of data owners, it could no longer be regarded as private as it ever was.[4]. On the other hand, while CSPs primarily achieve data protection through mechanisms such as firewalls and virtualization, these steps do not completely protect against risks of unauthorized access to data from insiders, outsiders, or other cloud tenants to non-bug-free installation and low degree of transparency. Infamous examples of data breach occur from time to time, such as the latest data breach by Sony PlayStation [5] and Drobox privacy leakage [6].

Cloud storage security: data storage, where end users can outsource any number of data cloud servers to enjoy pretty much unlimited hardware/software resources and key management, without or little investment, is one of the most used cloud services. Indeed, several well-known cloud service providers, including Microsoft SkyDrive, have started offering these services in the last few years [7] Amazon S3 [8], Dropbox [9], Apple iCloud, and Google Drive [2]. There are two significant features that complicate the development of data security technologies in the cloud: - Through a network of service providers, a private cloud can be provided. This means that the main supplier uses the services of other providers. (the identity to these indirect providers may be unknown to the user). This makes outsourced files more responsive to data mining and attacks [4] - It is also important to consider any possible changes to the indirect suppliers involved in a cloud service. For example, a participating provider could need to move its services to someone else along with user data because of the sale of the company, a merger, government seizure, etc. This means that many disabled hard drives can remain in the user's files even after the user's request to remove or close the account [4].

Cloud Virtualization is the other option that is most viable because it is another important aspect of cloud computing and has support in system development. Virtualization is a technology which abstracts physical hardware details and provides high-level applications with virtualized resources. A virtualized server is typically referred to as a virtual device (VM). The basis of cloud computing is virtualization, as it offers the ability to pool computing resources from form based and automatically allocate or dismiss virtual resources to on-demand applications. By facilitating virtual machine migration to balance load across the data center, virtualization can provide major advantages in cloud computing. Furthermore, migration of virtual machines allows robust and highly sensitive procurement in Centers of data [10]. Transition of the virtual machine has developed from process migration techniques [11]. More recently, Xen [12] and VMWare [13] have implemented “live” migration of VMs that involves extremely short downtimes ranging from tens of milliseconds to a second. Clark et al. [14] pointed out that migrating an entire OS and all its applications as one unit allows to avoid many of the difficulties faced by process level migration approaches and analyzed the benefits of live migration of VMs.

In current, Cloud Computing turns the provision of IT services upside down. It is therefore not surprising that Cloud Computing affects only its users, including from businesses to consumers to public administration, as well as the ICT industry and society and the economy. While there are many estimates of the effects of cloud computing on its users. There is hardly robust evidence of their environment. There is in addition, a very emotional and divisive discussion [15]. The use of cloud computing (CC) applications has increased the volume, speed and/or variety of data from different resources and applications produced every minute around the world. The data generated may be in the form of data that is structured, semi-structured or unstructured. It is stored in various formats that cannot be easily integrated at high speeds due to high data flow [16, 17]. Although data has grown from different fields on a large scale, companies face challenges and pressure to retain their place in the competitive market [17]. Compatible technologies are important for a new age of digital transformation, although current capabilities may not be able to manage them well. To succeed in the emerging digital market, companies would need to solve the complexities of big data [16]. New technologies are created thanks to cloud computing such as IoT, Blockchain, AI. thanks to these new technologies many more jobs have been created in different fields. Previous statistical surveys and system reviews have identified previous innovations, but innovations in the field of cloud computing require an analysis of the cloud computing driving paradigms (IoT, AI, and Blockchain). About emerging paradigms and technologies such as IoT, AI and Blockchain, there is a requirement for a systematic review to evaluate, upgrade and integrate existing research presented in this field [18].

Area 2: Cybersecurity and privacy

The broad implementation of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) brings many advantages to the smart grid, including enhanced efficiency and power quality, self-healing and increased resistance to interruption, predictive and automated maintenance or increased customer choice [19]. At the same time, the reliance on ICT and interconnection with the Internet is leading to new questions about security and privacy. Each network link of the grid is the possible entry for a cyber-attack and each network layer and the technologies used can become its possible target. In addition, since the smart grid is a complex system of distributed and interconnected systems, the attack surface is exceptionally large [20]. In the first-place security experts believe that structured solutions and practices should be used [21], [22]. Standards allow the officers responsible for the introduction of new security measures to address concerns about the choice of approaches, the objectives and nature of implementation, or the adequacy of the solution. Depending on the expertise and knowledge of the experts, solutions based on so-called' expert knowledge of individual company employees will suffer from constraints.[23]. Cybersecurity includes the awareness of the surrounding problems of different cyber attacks and the development of defense strategies (i.e., countermeasures) that protect the privacy, integrity, and availability of any digital and information technology [24].

- The word used to avoid the disclosure of information to unauthorized persons or systems is confidentiality.

- Integrity is the term used in an illegal way to avoid any modification/deletion.

- Availability is the term used to ensure that when needed and by those who require it the systems responsible for providing, storing and processing information are available.

Many experts in cybersecurity believe that malware is the key choice for malicious intent to breach cybersecurity efforts in cyberspace [25]. Malware refers to a broad class of attacks loaded on a system to compromise the system to the benefit of an adversary, typically without the knowledge of the legitimate owner. Viruses, worms, Trojan horses, spyware, and bot executables are some examples of malware [26].

Cybersecurity is the "body of combined technologies, processes and practices to protect data and networks against attacks, damage or unauthorized access" [27]. Cybercrime has a major negative effect on the global economy, with annual costs of around US$ 445 billion[28]. Cyber breaches through third party providers can also have an impact on an organization's overall financial performance and the value of its shareholders [29]. In the past, the manufacturing systems were closed, and their isolation and physical access control ensured security. Today, in contrast, modern manufacturing machines are equipped with several smart devices and are connected to other machines and data processing systems via wireless networks or wired Ethernet. Manufacturing components communicate using specific protocols over private industrial networks, but these do not provide adequate protection against cyber threats [30]. We used a "problematization" approach to evaluate government, industry and hackers' current conceptual frameworks of the cybersecurity problem. Our research showed that individual human actors are generally considered to be a problem" in a variety of roles. We also found that the solutions deployed focus mainly on preventing adverse events by building resistance: i.e., implementing new layers of security and policies that monitor people and limit their problem behavior. This treats all individuals in the system, in essence, as if they could well be malicious actors, and the solutions are designed to prevent their ill-advised behaviors [31]
